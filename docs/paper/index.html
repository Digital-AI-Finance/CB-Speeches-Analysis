Central Bank Communication and Macroeconomic Conditions

A PCA-Based Framework for Analyzing Narrative-Reality Disconnect

Gabin Taibi and Joerg Osterrieder
University of Zurich, 2025

Abstract: This paper presents a comprehensive empirical analysis of the relationship between Federal Reserve speech sentiment and macroeconomic conditions over three decades (1996-2025). Using Principal Component Analysis to construct interpretable macro indices and PELT-based structural break detection, we document a near-zero correlation (r = 0.005) between hawkish/dovish sentiment and underlying economic fundamentals. Our findings challenge conventional assumptions about the information content of central bank communication and have implications for market participants and policymakers alike.
